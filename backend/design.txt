All fire suppression strategies target one corner: sprinklers remove heat, foam excludes oxygen, isolation removes fuel.</p><div class="pt-tags"><span class="tag tip">Remove any one corner and the fire extinguishes</span><span class="tag code">SCDF Fire Code 2023 Ch.1 &mdash; Definitions</span></div></div></div><div class="pt exp" id="kp-w1-0-1"><div class="pt-hd" onclick="tog('kp-w1-0-1')"><span class="pt-bul" style="color:#005D6C">&#9658;</span><span class="pt-txt">Fire Tetrahedron adds the <b>Chemical Chain Reaction</b> as a fourth element</span><span class="pt-chev">&#10095;</span></div><div class="pt-body" style="border-left-color:#005D6C;background:white"><p class="pt-desc">The tetrahedron adds the <b>uninhibited chemical chain reaction</b> &mdash; free radicals (OH&middot;, H&middot;, O&middot;) sustaining combustion in a self-perpetuating cycle. Sprinkler systems are the primary defence. Most Singapore commercial spaces are primarily Class A hazard.</p><div class="pt-tags"><span class="tag sg">Most Singapore commercial buildings are primarily Class A hazard</span><span class="tag code">SS CP 52:2004 &mdash; LH and OH hazard classification</span></div></div></div><div class="pt exp" id="kp-w1-0-3"><div class="pt-hd" onclick="tog('kp-w1-0-3')"><span class="pt-bul" style="color:#005D6C">&#9658;</span><span class="pt-txt"><b>Class B:</b> flammable liquids and gases (petrol, solvents, LPG)</span><span class="pt-chev">&#10095;</span></div><div class="pt-body" style="border-left-color:#005D6C;background:white"><p class="pt-desc">Class B fires involve flammable liquids (petrol, diesel, solvents) and flammable gases (LPG, methane). <b>Water must NOT be applied to liquid Class B fires</b> &mdash; it spreads the burning liquid and can cause violent steam explosions. If equipment can be de-energised, the fire converts to Class A and water-based suppression then becomes appropriate.</p><div class="pt-tags"><span class="tag warn">NEVER use water on live electrical fires &mdash; electrocution risk</span><span class="tag tip">De-energise first &rarr; converts to Class A &rarr; water then safe</span></div></div></div><div class="pt exp" id="kp-w1-0-5"><div class="pt-hd" onclick="tog('kp-w1-0-5')"><span class="pt-bul" style="color:#005D6C">&#9658;</span><span class="pt-txt"><b>Class D:</b> combustible metals (Mg, Na, Li)</span><span class="pt-chev">&#10095;</span></div><div class="pt-body" style="border-left-color:#005D6C;background:white"><p class="pt-desc">Class D fires involve combustible metals (magnesium, sodium, lithium) burning at extreme temperatures and reacting violently with water.
